B-Tree Construction Breakdown

A continuous, step-by-step visual guide to building a B-Tree of Order 5 using alphabetical data.

Theory & Rules

Setup & Core Properties

Goal: Construct a B-tree of order 5 with the data:
D, H, Z, K, B, P, Q, E, A, S, W, T, C, L, N, Y, M

  • Order (m): 5
  • Maximum Children: m = 5
  • Maximum Keys per Node: m - 1 = 4
  • Minimum Keys per Node (Internal): ⌈m/2⌉ - 1 = ⌈2.5⌉ - 1 = 3 - 1 = 2
  • Sorting Rule: Data is ALWAYS inserted in ascending alphabetical order.
  • Growth Rule: Insertions happen at the leaf nodes. The tree grows upwards when a node splits.

Step 2: Split 1

Insert B (The First Split)

Attempting to insert B alphabetically yields: [B, D, H, K, Z]. This exceeds our max key limit of 4. We must split!

  • Find the median element: H
  • Push H up to become the new root.
  • Elements to the left `[B, D]` become the left child.
  • Elements to the right `[K, Z]` become the right child.

Step 9: Double Split!

Insert M (The Climax)

M belongs in the middle child `[K, L, N, P]`. Sequence [K, L, M, N, P] overflows.

  1. Find median of child: M. Push M up to the root.
  2. CRITICAL ISSUE: The root is already full (`[C, H, Q, W]`). Pushing M up creates [C, H, M, Q, W]. The root overflows!
  3. We must perform a Double Split. Find the median of the overflowing root: M
  4. M moves up to become the brand new Root node. The tree grows in height!
  5. Split the old root into left `[C, H]` and right `[Q, W]`.
  6. Re-attach all leaf nodes to their appropriate parents based on alphabetical ranges.

Verification

Instructor's Final Checklist

At the end of the video, the instructor verifies the tree to ensure no B-tree rules were broken:

  • ✅ Sorted Data: All data inside every node is in alphabetical (ascending) order.
  • ✅ Leaf Level: All leaf nodes are at the exact same depth/level.
  • ✅ Minimum Keys: The minimum number of keys a non-root node can have is 2. Every single leaf node and internal node has exactly 2 keys.
  • ✅ Maximum Keys: No node exceeds 4 keys.
