Evaluation of Postfix Expression

Instructor: Jenny (Jenny's Lectures CS/IT) • In-depth Study Notes

1. The Core Algorithm

To evaluate a postfix expression, we use a Stack data structure. We scan the expression strictly from left to right, one character at a time.

Begin
  for each character in postfix expr, do
    if operand (number) is encountered:
       push it onto Stack
    
    else if operator (+, -, *, /) is encountered:
       pop 2 elements from the stack
       A -> top element
       B -> Next to top element
       
       result = B operator A
       push result onto Stack
       
  return element of Stack top
End

🚨 The "Golden Rule" (Crucial for Exams)

Jenny strongly emphasizes the order of operations when you pop elements:

  • The first element popped (A) is the right operand.
  • The second element popped (B) is the left operand.

DO NOT DO A operator B. It must be B operator A. For example, if you pop 2 and then 6, and the operator is /, the calculation must be 6 / 2, not 2 / 6.

4. Study Summary & Checklist

  • ✅ Always scan the expression from Left to Right.
  • ✅ Numbers (Operands) go straight into the Stack.
  • ✅ Symbols (Operators) mean you pull two numbers out of the Stack.
  • ✅ Order of Operations Matter: The second number you pulled out goes first in the math equation (Second Number [Operator] First Number).
  • ✅ At the end of the expression, only one number should be left in your stack. That is your final evaluated answer.
