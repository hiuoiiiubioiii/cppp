Infix to Postfix Conversion

Complete Video Breakdown & Visual Study Guide

Using Stack (LIFO: Last In, First Out)

1. Core Concepts

Infix Expression: Operators are written between operands (e.g., A + B).

Postfix Expression: Operators are written after operands (e.g., A B +).

The Data Structure: We use a Stack to hold operators and parentheses. A stack strictly follows LIFO (Last In, First Out), meaning whatever goes in last comes out first.

2. The 8 Golden Rules

Rule 1: Operands

Print operands (letters/numbers like A, B, C) directly to the output as they arrive. Do not put them in the stack.

Rule 2: Empty Stack / Left Parenthesis on Top

If the stack is empty, or if the top of the stack is a left parenthesis (, push the incoming operator directly onto the stack.

Rule 3: Incoming Left Parenthesis '('

If the incoming symbol is a left parenthesis (, push it onto the stack. It acts as a marker.

Rule 4: Incoming Right Parenthesis ')'

Pop the stack and print the operators until a left parenthesis ( is found. Discard both the left and right parentheses.

Rule 5: Higher Precedence

If the incoming operator has a higher precedence than the top of the stack, simply push it onto the stack.

Rule 6: Lower Precedence

If the incoming symbol has a lower precedence than the top of the stack:
1. Pop & print the top.
2. Test the incoming operator against the *new* top of the stack (repeat popping if necessary).

Rule 7: Equal Precedence (Associativity Rule)

If the incoming operator has equal precedence to the top of the stack, check associativity:

  • Left to Right (L to R): Pop & print the top of the stack, then push the incoming operator. (Applies to +, -, *, /)
  • Right to Left (R to L): Push the incoming operator directly onto the stack. (Applies to exponents ^)

Rule 8: End of Expression

At the end of the expression, pop and print all remaining operators from the stack.
