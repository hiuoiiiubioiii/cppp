🌳 Construct a Binary Tree

In-depth Lecture Notes • Pre-order & In-order Traversals

1. Initial Setup & Given Data

To construct a binary tree, you must be given two sets of traversal data. Here is the data written on the whiteboard:

Pre-order: 1, 2, 4, 8, 9, 10, 11, 5, 3, 6, 7

In-order: 8, 4, 10, 9, 11, 2, 5, 1, 6, 3, 7

The "Golden Rules" (Memorize These)

  • Pre-order Formula: (Root, Left, Right)
    Why it matters: Scanning this array from left-to-right will always reveal the Root of whatever subtree you are currently working on.
  • In-order Formula: (Left, Root, Right)
    Why it matters: Once you find the root, locate it in this array. Everything to the left is the Left Subtree, and everything to the right is the Right Subtree.
Step 1

Finding the Main Root

Logic: In Pre-order traversal, the absolute root of the entire tree is traversed first. We look at the first item in the Pre-order array.

Pre-order: 1, 2, 4, 8, 9, 10, 11, 5, 3, 6, 7

Action: 1 is our main root. Now, we locate 1 in the In-order array to split the rest of the numbers into left and right sides of the tree.

In-order: 8, 4, 10, 9, 11, 2, 5, 1, 6, 3, 7
Step 2

Building the Left Subtree (Root '2')

Logic: We isolate the left elements 8, 4, 10, 9, 11, 2, 5. Which one is the root of this sub-group? We scan the original Pre-order list from left to right. The first number from this group that appears is our new root.

Pre-order: 1, 2, 4, 8, 9, 10, 11, 5... (2 appears first)

Action: 2 is the root of the left subtree. We find 2 in our isolated In-order sequence to split it again.

In-order: 8, 4, 10, 9, 11, 2, 5

Observation: Because 5 is completely alone on the right side of 2, 5 becomes the direct right child of 2.

Step 3

Continuing the Left Subtree (Root '4')

Logic: We now look at the remaining left elements: 8, 4, 10, 9, 11. Scanning the Pre-order array again, which of these appears first?

Pre-order: ...2, 4, 8, 9... (4 appears first)

Action: 4 is the root. Find it in the In-order sequence.

In-order: 8, 4, 10, 9, 11

Observation: Because 8 is completely alone on the left side of 4, 8 becomes the direct left child of 4.

Step 4

Finishing the Left Subtree (Root '9')

Logic: We have three elements left to place on the left side of the main tree: 10, 9, 11 (These are to the right of 4). Which one is the root?

Pre-order: ...8, 9, 10, 11... (9 appears first)

Action: 9 is the root. Find it in the In-order sequence.

In-order: 10, 9, 11

Observation: 10 is alone on the left (left child of 9), and 11 is alone on the right (right child of 9). The entire left side of the main tree is now complete!

Step 5

Building the Right Subtree (Root '3')

Logic: We return all the way to Step 1, where we separated the main Right Subtree elements: 6, 3, 7. Scanning the Pre-order array for these numbers, which appears first?

Pre-order: ... 3, 6, 7 (3 appears first)

Action: 3 is the root of the right side. Find it in the In-order sequence.

In-order: 6, 3, 7

Observation: 6 is alone on the left (left child of 3), and 7 is alone on the right (right child of 3). The tree is fully constructed!

✅ The Verification Trick

At the end of the video, the instructor provides a highly valuable exam tip to guarantee your answer is 100% correct.

How to verify your work:

Once you finish drawing your tree, pretend the original question doesn't exist. Manually calculate the Pre-order and In-order traversals based purely on your drawing. If your calculated sequences match the ones given in the original question, your tree is completely accurate!
