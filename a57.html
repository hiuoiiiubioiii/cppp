Constructing a Binary Tree from Preorder & Postorder

In-depth visual notes from Jenny's Lectures. Scroll down to learn the formal recursive method, the quick exam trick, and the proof of why standard Binary Trees aren't unique!

🛑 The Two Golden Rules

Before doing any calculations, you must memorize these two theoretical rules for your exams:

Rule 1: If you are ONLY given Preorder and Postorder traversals, it is IMPOSSIBLE to construct a UNIQUE Binary Tree. (Multiple trees can share the same sequences).
Rule 2: However, it IS POSSIBLE to construct a UNIQUE Full Binary Tree from Preorder and Postorder traversals. (A Full Binary tree is where every node has either 0 or 2 children).

*We will prove both of these rules at the end of this feed.

🛠️ Method 1: The Formal (Recursive) Method

This method involves repeatedly slicing the arrays into Left Subtrees (LST) and Right Subtrees (RST) until the tree is built.

1

Find the Main Root: As identified above, the root is F.

2

Identify the root of the Left Subtree: Look at the element immediately following the root in Preorder. It is B.

3

Split the arrays: Find B in the Postorder array. Everything to the left of B (inclusive) forms the Left Subtree. Everything after B forms the Right Subtree.

🚀 Method 2: The "Simple Trick" (Exam Hack)

The instructor highly recommends this visual trick for exams to save time without writing out sub-arrays.

The Trick: Read elements one-by-one left-to-right from the Preorder array. Locate that element in the Postorder array.

If the new element is to the LEFT of its parent in Postorder, it is a child node. Always try to fill the Left child slot first. If the left slot is taken, fill the Right child slot.
  • Start with Root F.
  • Next in Preorder is B. In Postorder, B is left of F ➡️ B becomes Left child of F.
  • Next is A. In Postorder, A is left of B ➡️ A becomes Left child of B.
  • Next is D. In Postorder, D is right of A but left of B. Because B's left child is already taken by A ➡️ D becomes Right child of B.
  • Next is C. In Postorder, C is left of D ➡️ C becomes Left child of D.
  • Next is E. In Postorder, E is left of D, but D's left slot is taken by C ➡️ E becomes Right child of D.
  • Next is G. In Postorder, G is left of F. F's left is taken by B ➡️ G becomes Right child of F.
  • Next is I. In Postorder, I is left of G ➡️ I becomes Left child of G.
  • Next is H. In Postorder, H is left of I ➡️ H becomes Left child of I.

🤯 Proof: Why isn't it Unique? (Rule 1)

To prove that standard Binary Trees can't be uniquely built from Pre/Postorder, look at nodes G and I. They only have left children. What happens if we move those children to the right side?

The Mind-Blower: If you calculate the Preorder and Postorder of the Modified Tree, they are exactly the same as the original arrays! Therefore, multiple tree shapes produce the same exact sequences.
