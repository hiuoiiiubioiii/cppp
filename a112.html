Problem 1: Decimals

The Question Statement

A text is made up of characters a, b, c, d, e each occurring with the probability 0.11, 0.40, 0.16, 0.09, and 0.24 respectively.

What is the average length of the optimal Huffman coding technique?

Instructor's Rule #1 Always build the Huffman tree from the bottom up by repeatedly selecting the two minimum probabilities available in your current pool. Put the smaller on the left, larger on the right.
Visualization 1

Step 1: Building the Decimal Tree

Let's map out exactly what she drew on the whiteboard:

  1. Merge minimums d(0.09) + a(0.11) = 0.20
  2. Merge new min c(0.16) + 0.20 = 0.36
  3. Merge new min e(0.24) + 0.36 = 0.60
  4. Merge last min b(0.40) + 0.60 = 1.00
Crucial Checkpoint The root node must always equal 1.00 when using full probabilities. If it doesn't, you have made a math error during addition!
Calculations

Step 2 & 3: Assign Codes & Calculate Average

Assign a 0 to every left branch and a 1 to every right branch. Trace the path from the top root down to the letter.

a0.111 1 1 14
b0.4001

The Formula: Average Length = Sum of (Probability × Number of Bits)

Avg = (0.11 × 4) + (0.40 × 1) + (0.16 × 3) + (0.09 × 4) + (0.24 × 2)

Avg = 0.44 + 0.40 + 0.48 + 0.36 + 0.48

Avg = 2.16 bits per symbol
🔥 Exam Hacks

Instructor's Pro Tips for Exams

Time management is everything in NET/GATE. Here is how you speed this up:

  1. Skip the Table: During an exam, do not draw the large table she drew on the board. Just draw the tree, trace the lengths visually, and write the math equation directly to save time.
  2. The 100 Characters Trick: Sometimes the exam asks "How many total bits are required to represent 100 characters?" Since your average is bits per symbol, simply multiply your final answer by 100.
    2.16 × 100 = 216 total bits
Problem 2: Fractions

Handling Fractional Probabilities

Given symbols A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H with probabilities 1/30, 1/30, 1/30, 2/30, 3/30, 5/30, 5/30, 12/30 respectively. What is the average Huffman code size in bits per symbol?

Instructor Tip: Do Not Simplify! Because the denominators are all exactly the same (30), do not simplify the fractions (e.g., don't turn 5/30 into 1/6). Leaving them as /30 makes finding minimums and adding much faster. Just look at the numerators!

Final Step

Direct Calculation (The Fast Way)

Instead of building a table, look at the tree visually and count how many "steps" (bits) it takes from the top root to reach each letter. Multiply that step count by the numerator.

  • H (12/30): 1 step → 12 × 1 = 12
  • F (5/30): 3 steps → 5 × 3 = 15
  • G (5/30): 3 steps → 5 × 3 = 15
  • E (3/30): 3 steps → 3 × 3 = 9
  • D (2/30): 4 steps → 2 × 4 = 8
  • C (1/30): 5 steps → 1 × 5 = 5
  • A (1/30): 6 steps → 1 × 6 = 6
  • B (1/30): 6 steps → 1 × 6 = 6
Add all numerators: (12 + 15 + 15 + 9 + 8 + 5 + 6 + 6) / 30

Avg = 76 / 30 bits per symbol
