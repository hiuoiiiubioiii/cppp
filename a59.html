Construction of BST (Preorder Given)

A continuous scrolling study guide based on the lecture breakdown

1. The Starting Information

The instructor notes that in exams, you are typically given a sequence and asked to either find other sequences or construct the tree. We are given the Preorder traversal.

Rule to remember: Preorder traversal always follows Root, Left, Right.

2. The Crucial Concept: Finding Inorder

To easily construct a tree, you need both the Preorder and Inorder sequences. Here is the instructor's biggest secret weapon:

The Inorder traversal of a Binary Search Tree (BST) will ALWAYS yield the data in ascending (sorted) order.

So, to find the Inorder sequence, simply take our Preorder numbers and sort them from smallest to largest. (Inorder traversal follows Left, Root, Right).

Inorder:
5
16
17
18
19
20
60
70
85

3. The "Golden Rule" for Construction

Throughout the rest of the problem, the instructor repeats a two-step mental loop to build the tree:

  1. Look at the Preorder list: Find the first number in the current subset. That number is the Root.
  2. Look at the Inorder list: Locate that Root. Everything to its left belongs in the Left Subtree. Everything to its right belongs in the Right Subtree.

4. Finding the Main Root

We look at our Preorder sequence. The very first number is 20. This is the main root of the entire tree.

We find 20 in the Inorder sequence. Everything left goes to the left subtree, everything right goes to the right subtree.

Inorder:
5
16
17
18
19
20
60
70
85

5. Constructing the Left Subtree (Part 1)

Focusing on the left subset: {5, 16, 17, 18, 19}. Which of these appears FIRST in our original Preorder list? It's 16. So 16 is our new root.

Looking at Inorder: 5 is to the left of 16. {17, 18, 19} are to the right.

6. Constructing the Left Subtree (Part 2)

Now focus on the subset: {17, 18, 19}. Which appears FIRST in the Preorder list? It's 18. It becomes the root.

In the Inorder list, 17 is left of 18, and 19 is right of 18. This completes the entire left side of the tree.

7. Constructing the Right Subtree (Part 1)

We jump back up to the right side of the main root 20. The subset is {60, 70, 85}.

Checking the Preorder list, 60 appears first. It is the root.

In the Inorder list, both 70 and 85 are to the right of 60. There is nothing to its left, meaning 60 has no left child.

8. Constructing the Right Subtree (Part 2)

Final subset: {70, 85}. In the Preorder list, 85 appears first.

In the Inorder list, 70 is located to the left of 85. Therefore, 70 is the left child of 85.

9. The Final Binary Search Tree

By strictly following the Golden Rule (Root from Preorder, Left/Right splits from Inorder), the instructor completes the tree.
