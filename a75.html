Constructing a B-Tree

Mastering the step-by-step insertion algorithm

📋 The Foundational Rules (Memorize These!)

  • Sequence: 5, 3, 21, 9, 1, 13, 2, 7, 10, 12, 4, 8
  • Order (m): 4 (A node can have a max of 4 children)
  • Max Keys per Node: m - 1 = 3
  • Sorting Rule: Keys inside a node are ALWAYS sorted in ascending order.
  • Insertion Rule: You MUST insert new numbers into a leaf node at the bottom.

Insert 9 — FIRST SPLIT!

If we insert 9, the sorted array would temporarily be [3, 5, 9, 21]. This is 4 keys, which breaks our rule! We must perform a split.

👩‍🏫 Instructor Insight: Left-Biased vs Right-Biased
When splitting 4 numbers, there is no single middle number. The middle elements are 5 and 9. If we push 5 up, we are creating a Left-Biased tree. If we push 9 up, it's a Right-Biased tree. Both are mathematically correct, but you must pick one and stick to it! Let's choose Left-Biased and push 5 up.

The Action: The 5 moves up to become the new root. The 3 becomes the left child, and 9 and 21 become the right child.

Insert 4 — CASCADING SPLIT!

Traversing down, 4 goes to the far-left node: [1 | 2 | 3]. Overflow occurs: [1 | 2 | 3 | 4]. The median 2 pushes UP to the root.

Uh Oh! The root was already full! Pushing 2 up creates a temporary root of [2 | 5 | 9 | 12]. We have to split the root!

👩‍🏫 Instructor Insight: The Cascading Split
When a parent node overflows because a child pushed a number up, we simply apply the exact same rule. Find the median of the root [2 | 5 | 9 | 12], which is 5. We push 5 UP to become a brand new root level, increasing the height of the entire tree!
