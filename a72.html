Constructing a B-Tree Step-by-Step

Order 3 • Inserting values 1 through 10

📌 1. The Core Rules & Properties

Before drawing anything, the instructor defines the mathematical boundaries of the tree based on its "Order". This dictates the size of our nodes.

🧠 The Golden Formulas

Order (m) = 3

Max Children per node = m = 3

Max Keys per node = (m - 1) = 3 - 1 = 2

Because the Max Keys is 2, every node we draw will have two "slots" for numbers. Values are always inserted in sorted order, and insertions always happen at the leaf nodes (bottom).

Steps 1 & 2

Insert 1 and 2

We start with an empty root node. We insert 1. Then we insert 2.

Since the node has a maximum capacity of 2 keys, the node is now full. No splits are needed yet.

Step 3

Insert 3 (The First Split)

We try to insert 3. The node becomes [ 1 | 2 | 3 ].

Overflow Alert! A node can only hold 2 keys. We must apply the Split Rule:

  1. Find the Median (middle) element. Here, the median is 2.
  2. Push the median one level up to create a new root.
  3. Split the remaining items to the left and right.

💡 Instructor Note

"A B-Tree always grows upwards. The leaf nodes will always remain at the exact same level."

Steps 4 & 5

Insert 4 and 5 (The Second Split)

Insert 4: Start at Root (2). 4 is greater than 2, so go Right. Insert into the node containing 3. Node becomes [ 3 | 4 ].

Insert 5: Start at Root (2). 5 is greater than 2, so go Right. Try to insert into [ 3 | 4 ].

Overflow Alert! Node becomes [ 3 | 4 | 5 ]. The median is 4. Push 4 up to the parent node. Split 3 (left) and 5 (right).

Step 7

Insert 7 (Double Overflow / Root Split)

This is the most complex step in the video!

Action 1: Try to insert 7. It goes to the bottom right node: [ 5 | 6 | 7 ]. Overflow.

Action 2: The median is 6. Push 6 up to the parent. The remaining 5 and 7 split.

Action 3: Parent Overflow! The parent node is the root [ 2 | 4 ]. Adding 6 makes it [ 2 | 4 | 6 ].

Action 4: Split the root! The median is 4. Push 4 up to create a brand new root node.

Look at how the tree has grown. Because we pushed a value up from the root, the entire height of the tree increased by 1 level. This ensures the tree stays perfectly balanced.

Steps 8 & 9

Insert 8 and 9

Insert 8: Follow path: Right of 4 → Right of 6 → Insert into [7]. Node becomes [ 7 | 8 ].

Insert 9: Follow path to [ 7 | 8 ]. Insert 9. Overflow: [ 7 | 8 | 9 ].

Split: Median is 8. Push 8 UP to its parent [ 6 ]. The parent becomes [ 6 | 8 ]. Split 7 and 9.

Step 10 (Final)

Insert 10

Follow the path: Right of 4 → Right of 8 (since 10 > 8) → down to the leaf node [ 9 ].

Insert 10 into the node. The node becomes [ 9 | 10 ]. There is space, so no overflow happens.

This is the final completed B-Tree!
